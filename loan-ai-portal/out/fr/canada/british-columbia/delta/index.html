Questions fréquentes

What is the maximum legal interest rate in Canada?

Under Section 347 of the Criminal Code of Canada, the maximum legal interest rate is 60% APR. Any lender charging more than 60% annual percentage rate is committing a criminal offense. Always verify the total cost of borrowing before signing.

How do I verify if a lender is licensed in Canada?

Check with your provincial regulator. Each province maintains a registry of licensed lenders. For banks, verify with OSFI (Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions). For credit unions, check provincial credit union regulators. Never borrow from unlicensed lenders.

What personal loan amounts are available in Canada?

Personal loans in Canada typically range from $1,000 to $50,000, depending on your credit score, income, and lender. Banks and credit unions offer the best rates for larger amounts ($10,000+), while alternative lenders may approve smaller loans ($1,000-$5,000) with less stringent credit requirements.

How long does it take to get approved for a loan in Canada?

Approval times vary by lender and loan type. Online lenders may approve within 24 hours with funds in 1-3 business days. Banks typically take 3-7 business days. Larger loans require more documentation and may take 1-2 weeks. Have your SIN, proof of income, and ID ready to speed up the process.

Will applying for a loan affect my credit score in Canada?

Yes, but minimally. Each loan application typically results in a "hard inquiry" that may lower your credit score by 5-10 points temporarily. Multiple applications within 14-45 days (rate shopping window) are usually treated as a single inquiry. Soft credit checks for pre-qualification do not affect your score.
